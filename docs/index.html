Urban Amenities & Housing Price Inequality in Seoul

Exploring the spatial relationship between urban infrastructure and housing price growth across Seoul's 25 districts.

Hakyun Song Insung Lee Taesoon Kim

📋 Overview

About This Project

Housing prices in Seoul have surged over the past decade, but growth rates vary significantly across the city's 25 districts. This study analyzes how urban amenities — safety, transportation, education, and green space — explain these price disparities.

🔬 Methodology

Three-Step Analytical Framework

01

Descriptive Spatial Analysis

Choropleth maps visualize housing price growth rates by district, revealing spatial concentration patterns. Time-series trends compare sale vs. rent dynamics.

Choropleth Maps Time-Series Spatial Clustering
02

Correlation & Regression

A multivariate regression estimates the statistical relationship between price growth and amenity indicators: Price = β₀ + β₁Safety + β₂Transport + β₃Education + β₄Green + ε

OLS Regression Effect Sizes Statistical Significance
03

Cluster Analysis

K-means clustering groups districts by similarities in price and amenity profiles, identifying structural typologies.

K-Means District Typology Pattern Recognition
