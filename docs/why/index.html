🔍 Background

Why We Chose This Topic

Understanding the motivations and urgency behind studying Seoul's housing price inequality.

🏠

Housing Crisis in Seoul

Seoul's apartment prices have surged over 50% in the past decade, making homeownership increasingly unattainable for young generations and middle-income families.

📊

Growing Inequality

The gap between the most expensive and cheapest districts has widened dramatically, with Gangnam-area prices now 3–4× higher than northern districts like Dobong or Nowon.

🌍

Global Relevance

Seoul's housing dynamics mirror challenges in cities like London, San Francisco, and Hong Kong — offering lessons for urban policy worldwide.

🏙️

Amenity-Driven Pricing

Access to quality schools, subway stations, parks, and safety infrastructure appears to be a key driver of price disparities — an under-explored angle in Korean housing research.

⚖️

Policy Debate

Korea has implemented over 25 real estate regulations since 2017, yet prices continued to rise — raising urgent questions about policy effectiveness.

🎓

Academic Gap

Most studies focus on macro-level factors. Our research bridges this gap by combining macro analysis with micro-level amenity data at the district level.
