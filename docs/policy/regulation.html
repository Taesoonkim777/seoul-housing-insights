Major Policies Since 2017

Below is a summary of key housing policies implemented in Seoul, focusing on regulation, taxation, and supply-side interventions.

8.2 Housing Measures
Aug 2017
  • Designated Seoul as speculative overheating zone
  • Strengthened mortgage lending restrictions
  • Increased capital gains tax for multi-homeowners
  • Expanded resale restrictions
9.13 Measures
Sep 2018
  • Higher tax burden on multi-homeowners
  • Tightened housing loan regulations
  • Reformed subscription system toward end-users
12.16 Measures
Dec 2019
  • Mortgage ban for homes above KRW 1.5B
  • Stricter LTV rules
  • Increased tax on high-value properties
6.17 Measures
Jun 2020
  • Expanded regulated areas
  • Introduced land transaction permits
  • Tightened jeonse-loan usage rules
7.10 Measures
Jul 2020
  • Increased acquisition and holding taxes
  • Reduced rental housing tax benefits
  • Strengthened multi-homeowner regulation
8.4 Supply Plan
Aug 2020
  • Large-scale housing supply expansion
  • Public-led redevelopment projects
  • Focus on urban housing supply
