📊 Policy Evaluation

Assessment of major housing policy interventions and their effectiveness in Seoul

Policy Timeline

This timeline shows when major policies were implemented relative to housing price trends. It helps identify whether interventions aligned with market dynamics or lagged behind them.

Major Housing Policies (2017–2025)

The policies below illustrate how the government's approach evolved over time. Despite repeated interventions, structural challenges remained unresolved.

Phase 1. Initial Anti-Speculation Measures
8.2 Measures
Aug 2017
  • Speculative zone designation
  • Mortgage restrictions
  • Capital gains tax increase

Outcome: Temporary demand reduction.

Limitation: Prices rebounded quickly.

9.13 Measures
Sep 2018
  • Stronger taxation on multi-homeowners
  • Tighter loan regulation

Outcome: Reduced transaction volume.

Limitation: Limited effect on expectations.

Phase 2. Credit Tightening
12.16 Measures
Dec 2019
  • Mortgage ban for high-end housing
  • Stricter LTV rules

Outcome: High-end transactions declined.

Limitation: Mid-market prices continued rising.

6.17 Measures
Jun 2020
  • Expanded regulation zones
  • Land transaction permits

Outcome: Slowed activity locally.

Limitation: Spillover to nearby areas.

Phase 3. Tax & Supply Mix
7.10 Measures
Jul 2020
  • Higher taxes on multiple properties
  • Rental tax benefit reduction

Outcome: Increased holding costs.

Limitation: Did not expand supply.

8.4 Supply Plan
Aug 2020
  • Large-scale housing supply plan
  • Public-led development

Outcome: Improved supply expectations.

Limitation: Delayed implementation.

Phase 4. Public Supply & Deregulation
2.4 Supply Plan
Feb 2021
  • Public-led redevelopment
  • Fast-track approval

Outcome: Strengthened supply strategy.

Limitation: Local resistance slowed progress.

Normalization Policies
2022–2023
  • Deregulation and easing
  • Removal of control zones

Outcome: Transactions recovered.

Limitation: Risk of renewed price surge.

Phase 5. New Cycle (2024–2025)
Supply Expansion Package
Aug 2024
  • Accelerated housing projects
  • Urban supply expansion

Outcome: Supply expectations improved.

Limitation: Structural demand remained strong.

Loan Regulation Package
Jan 2025
  • Stricter mortgage rules
  • Combined supply-demand policy

Outcome: Targeted speculative demand.

Limitation: Reduced accessibility for genuine buyers.

Seoul Metropolitan Loan Tightening
Jun 2025
  • Tighter housing-backed loan screening
  • Stronger residency and repayment requirements

Outcome: Reinforced demand-side control in high-pressure areas.

Limitation: Did not address the underlying shortage of housing supply.

Additional Supply and LTV Measures
Sep 2025
  • Expanded housing supply commitments
  • Adjusted LTV rules in selected districts

Outcome: Combined supply support with tighter financial regulation.

Limitation: Frequent policy shifts increased market uncertainty.
