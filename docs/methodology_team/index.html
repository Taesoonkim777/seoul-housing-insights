Our research approach, data sources, analytical framework, and the team behind this project.

Methodology

01

Data Collection

Gathered apartment transaction data (sale, jeonse, monthly rent) from the Korea Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport, along with district-level amenity data from Seoul Open Data Portal.

MOLIT API Seoul Open Data 2015–2024
02

Amenity Index Construction

Built a composite urban amenity index incorporating transportation access (subway/bus), education quality, public safety (crime rates), and green space availability for each of Seoul's 25 districts.

PCA Z-score normalization Composite Index
03

Macro & Micro Analysis

Examined macroeconomic drivers (interest rates, household debt, supply volume) alongside micro-level amenity factors to understand their relative impact on housing prices.

Regression Correlation Time Series
04

Visualization & Storytelling

Created interactive visualizations using Plotly.js and D3.js to communicate findings through an accessible, narrative-driven web interface.

Plotly.js D3.js Scrollytelling

Data Sources

  • Apartment sale prices (2015–2024)

Seoul Open Data Portal & Public APIs

  • Subway station locations & ridership
  • School ratings & enrollment
  • Crime statistics by district
  • Park area & green space ratio

Macroeconomic Data

Bank of Korea & Statistics Korea

  • Base interest rate history
  • Household debt & mortgage data
  • Housing supply volume
  • Consumer price index
