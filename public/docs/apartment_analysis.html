🏢 Apartment Analysis

Analysis of Apartment Sale, Jeonse, and Rental Prices in Seoul with District-level Comparison

💰 Key Insights — Sales Price

  • Gangnam 3 Districts (Gangnam, Seocho, Songpa) consistently show the highest average sales prices, often 2–3× the city average.
  • Significant price gap between the northern (Gangbuk) and southern (Gangnam) areas of the Han River.
  • Districts near major business hubs and subway lines command premium prices due to accessibility and infrastructure.
  • Recent new construction in Mapo, Yongsan, and Dongjak has driven notable price increases in these areas.

🏠 Key Insights — Jeonse Deposit

  • Jeonse-to-sales price ratio varies significantly: lower in premium districts (40–50%) and higher in affordable areas (60–70%).
  • Districts with high demand from young professionals — such as Mapo, Seodaemun, and Gwanak — show elevated Jeonse prices relative to their sales prices.
  • The Gangnam cluster leads in absolute Jeonse deposits, reflecting the overall premium of the area.
  • Rising interest rates have recently caused a shift from Jeonse to monthly rent, softening Jeonse demand in some districts.

📋 Key Insights — Rental Price

  • Monthly rental prices are highest in Gangnam, Seocho, and Yongsan, driven by corporate demand and high-end housing stock.
  • University-adjacent districts like Gwanak and Seodaemun show high rental volumes but relatively moderate per-unit prices.
  • The rental market has expanded significantly as tenants shift away from large Jeonse deposits amid economic uncertainty.
  • Newer developments with premium amenities (gyms, co-working spaces) command 20–30% rental premiums over older buildings in the same district.
